STEMMINDS Corp - STEM Educator / Lead Game Developer - 05/2018 - 01/2020

  • At STEMMINDS I set out to learn how technology was being integrated with our education system. I wanted to make an impact as a technology educator and learn how to use a variety of different technologies. My goal was to inspire others to bring their ideas to life through teamwork, problem-solving, and applying technical skills.

  • I worked on a major government-funded project to develop software for the company's online STEM platform. This was a new initiative for the company that was aimed at giving STEM courses on robots, web technologies, and video game creation to elementary schools to make incorporating STEM into the curriculum easier for teachers.

  • The challenges I overcame for this project were:
    • learning how to use Unity to create the video games.
    • Working on a small team and learning how to collaborate on projects.
    • Leading teams of co-op students to build games using Unity.

  • Learning a new technology

  • To learn new technology quickly my approach is to understand why I need to use that particular tool. It comes from starting with a plan of what the product needs to do and visualizing the end result. From that, I can narrow down what tool would be best for the task and proceed to learn how to use it.

    For this project, Unity was the chosen solution for its ease of use, flexibility, and well-supported community. The way I got up to speed with Unity was by making small projects focusing on making individual features work on a small scale before scaling it up to the main project. My approach to learning a new piece of technology through reading and experimenting with small projects is how I am able to learn quickly and build faster.

  • Executing a plan and collaborating

  • Once I became more proficient with Unity, the next step was to create a plan of action and collaborate with the team towards building the software.

    The approach was to build a framework for each project that needed to be created. The templates outlined the details for the project and features that would be implemented. This approach made it easier for keeping track of our ideas and maintaining flexibility if changes needed to be made.

    When building the projects in Unity, I suggested that we test our collaboration through small projects to understand how to combine our work and keep things organized. We tested using Git for Unity to make code changes to scripts easier to manage and worked on separate scenes within the same project. This approach worked well on several of the projects that required different features. For example, I worked on the UI and controls for the player while my co-worker designed the level and interactions. When it came time to sync up our work we were able to combine our work to make a fully functional game.

  • Leadership and mentoring

  • Another aspect of the project was leading a team of co-op students to help complete other projects on the list. I took this as an opportunity to provide mentorship to students with limited experience in programming and help shape their perspective on what they can create when shown the possibilities.

    To bring the co-op team up to speed, I had them follow the same learning path I took when I started with Unity. The advantage here was I would be able to share the problems I came across when learning Unity. From this, they could get up and running relatively fast and start creating simple mechanics for the games my co-worker and I had templated. Throughout the learning and building process I also made sure to have open discussion sessions with the co-op team. A big part of the experience I wanted them to have for this project was getting them to share ideas and ask questions. For me, it was about being a mentor and sharing any helpful experiences that could inspire and educate.

    Overall the leadership I provided had a positive outcome for completing the goals for the project and inspiring the students.
